Skype Call Controls

Skype is a messaging and calling app that can be used to contact online and cellular users.

The team was stuck at what to offer as the solution to making control actions during a group video call, as there were several design options.

The PM had concerns on that some of the features (including existing features and new design) placed in menus being less findable.

My primary goal was to get user feedback to facilitate the designer's decision making on which solution to choose.

For the concern that the PM had, I sensed that a meaningful question to ask was: does the content structure of the app match what users expect in terms of hierarchy? For example, should 'take a screenshot' to be found in a 'more option' menu, or on the surface of the app that can be clicked right away?

1
Usability testing

As the priority was to validate design variants, it made sense to do a usability study. I conducted remote live usability testing with 6 participants on the new design solutions. Some solutions that had two options were counter-balanced.

2
Tree testing

To understand users expectation on content structure, I explore the tree testing method. I learned that it is a good method for testing textual content findability. There were some challenges to make this method work because mobile app content has visual information in addition to textual information. I piloted different ways of task design and settled on one with descriptive tree branch titles.

Success

The team agreed on the next move to make and delivered revised design after one month.

Lesson Learned

A lot of my projects prior to this one had simple research goals. Most of them were to validate new design changes and therefore a standard usability testing was sufficient. This time, I had research questions from different partners. While the center topic was aligned, the questions could not be tackled by one single approach. I explored and succeed in planning the research consisted of two research methods.
